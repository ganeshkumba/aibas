Data Storage & Forensic Transparency Protocol

How is my data stored?

The Ledger utilizes a Decoupled Storage Architecture. While transactional metadata resides in high-performance relational tables, your physical artifacts (Invoices, Receipts) are stored in business-level isolated vaults. Sensitve account identifiers are protected by rigorous access control lists (ACLs).

Is my data encrypted?

All Identity Tokens (Passwords) are cryptographically hashed using PBKDF2 with a dynamic salt, making them mathematically impossible to decrypt. Transactional data is moved through encrypted TLS 1.3 tunnels. For high-compliance requirements, we support Field-Level Encryption (FLE) for entities with active CA-Audit contracts.

How does the Forensic Shield work?

The engine performs Multivector Triangulation. It doesn't just look at the numbers; it analyzes the technical fingerprint of every upload (IP Geolocation, Hardware User-Agents, and PDF Producer Strings). If an employee creates an invoice on their local desktop and uploads it, the "Internal Metadata Signature" triggers an instant anomaly flag.

Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKP) are utilized for inter-entity synchronization, ensuring subsidiary data is verified without exposing raw ledger contents.
